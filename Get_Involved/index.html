We Need Your Help!

Mapping Prejudice has utilized optical character recognition (ORC) to identity over 30,000 property deeds containing racial language in Hennepin County. In order to reach our goal of mapping every racial covenant in Minneapolis, we need help transcribing these deed images.

To do this, we are leveraging Zooniverse. This digital platform enables community and volunteer based research by hosting content that can then be crowd-sourced. For Mapping Prejudice, this means hosting all deeds that OCR has identified as containing racial language. When you sign in to our Zooniverse project page, you can start transcribing these flagged images. For each image, you will be asked a series of questions. Each deed takes no more than a few minutes. The information that you enter will then be exported and used to finish our map of racial restrictions. With enough volunteer help, we hope to have mapped all of the racial covenants in Minneapolis by the end of the year.

Getting involved is easy.

1. Go to Zooniverse.org and set up an account
2. Watch our 5 minute tutorial video
3. Go to our Zooniverse project page and start annotating

As a volunteer, you will have the opportunity to:
read critical historical records
document how private contracts undergirded structural racism in Minneapolis
contribute to a community resource that can fuel future conversations about racial disparities and housing policies

There are also two Zooniverse training sessions every week. We highly recommend coming to one of these sessions before you start annotating. Click on the "events" tab at the top of the page for times and locations.

We are working to shift the collective conversation around race. Please join us as we work to illuminate the workings of structural racism.
